About

I joined RazorMetrics in 2021 as the company's first and only DevOps engineer. Back then it was a team of seven with no live customers, running on a single-account CloudFormation and EC2 stack. Over the next five years I watched it grow into a 60-plus-person engineering organization serving multiple enterprise health-plan and pharmacy clients and I rebuilt the platform underneath that grew into a multi-account, containerized, Terraform-driven system. Each major migration, from CloudFormation to Terraform, EC2 to Fargate, CodeCommit to Bitbucket and monolith toward microservices, came from a real shift in what the business needed as the customers, load and compliance demands scaled.

I have seen firsthand how an organization's infrastructure needs change as it matures, how to adapt without stalling delivery and how processes mature as startups grow. I turn complex cloud challenges into resilient architecture, automate the workflows that slow teams down and build security-first practices into everything. Whether that is infrastructure as code, containerized applications, monitoring that tells you what is actually wrong or CI/CD that just works, I care about solutions that are genuinely useful for the people who depend on them.
